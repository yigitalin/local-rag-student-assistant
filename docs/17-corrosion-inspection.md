# Corrosion Inspection and Treatment

## Purpose

Procedures for identifying, assessing, and treating corrosion on refinery process equipment and piping systems.

---

## Safety Warning

- Corroded components may have reduced pressure containment capability.
- Do NOT apply mechanical cleaning methods on pressurized or energized equipment.
- Assess wall thickness before making any repair decision.

---

## Types of Corrosion

### External Corrosion

- General surface corrosion (uniform wall loss)
- Pitting corrosion (localized deep pits)
- Crevice corrosion (under deposits, gaskets, or bolted joints)
- Microbiologically Influenced Corrosion (MIC)

### Internal Corrosion

- Erosion-corrosion caused by process flow
- CO₂ corrosion (sweet corrosion)
- H₂S corrosion (sour corrosion)

---

## Inspection Procedure

1. Clean the inspection area to bare metal.
2. Perform a visual inspection and photograph all defects.
3. Measure wall thickness using ultrasonic testing (UT) at four clock positions.
4. Measure pit depth using a calibrated pit depth gauge.
5. Classify the severity:

| Category | Wall Loss | Required Action |
|----------|----------:|-----------------|
| Minor | <10% | Monitor during the next scheduled inspection |
| Moderate | 10–30% | Increase monitoring frequency and plan repair |
| Severe | 30–50% | Engineering assessment and repair within 90 days |
| Critical | >50% | Immediate isolation and emergency repair |

---

## Treatment Options

### Surface Preparation

1. Remove loose scale using non-sparking tools.
2. Perform abrasive blasting to Sa 2.5 (where permitted).
3. Clean oil and grease contamination using an approved solvent.

### Coating Repair

1. Apply primer within 4 hours after surface preparation.
2. Apply intermediate coating according to the manufacturer's specification.
3. Apply the finish coat to achieve the required dry film thickness (DFT).
4. Allow the coating to cure before returning the equipment to service.

### Composite Repair (Clock Spring or Equivalent)

1. Apply only to localized external corrosion without leakage.
2. Clean and prepare the damaged surface.
3. Install the composite repair system according to the manufacturer's instructions.
4. Allow the required curing time (typically 2–4 hours at ambient temperature).
5. Record the repair location and date in the asset register.
