# Corrosion Inspection and Treatment

## Purpose
Procedures for identifying, assessing, and treating corrosion on gas infrastructure components.

## Safety Warning
- Corroded components may have reduced pressure containment capability.
- Do NOT apply mechanical cleaning methods on pressurised or live equipment.
- Assess wall thickness before any repair decision.

## Types of Corrosion

### External Corrosion
- General surface corrosion (uniform wall loss)
- Pitting corrosion (localised deep pits)
- Crevice corrosion (under deposits, gaskets, or bolts)
- Microbiologically influenced corrosion (MIC)

### Internal Corrosion
- Erosion-corrosion from flow conditions
- CO₂ corrosion (sweet corrosion)
- H₂S corrosion (sour corrosion)

## Inspection Procedure
1. Clean surface to bare metal at inspection points.
2. Visual assessment – photograph all defects.
3. UT wall thickness measurements at 4 clock positions.
4. Pit depth gauge for individual pits.
5. Classify severity:

| Category | Wall Loss | Action |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Minor | <10% | Monitor – next scheduled inspection |
| Moderate | 10–30% | Increased monitoring, plan repair |
| Severe | 30–50% | Engineering assessment, repair within 90 days |
| Critical | >50% | Immediate isolation, emergency repair |

## Treatment Options

### Surface Preparation
1. Remove loose scale with wire brush (non-sparking tools on gas plant).
2. Abrasive blast to SA 2.5 (if hot work permit allows).
3. Solvent clean for grease/oil contamination.

### Coating Repair
1. Apply primer within 4 hours of surface preparation.
2. Apply intermediate coat per manufacturer spec.
3. Apply topcoat – minimum total DFT per design.
4. Allow full cure time before backfill (if buried).

### Composite Repair (Clock Spring or equivalent)
1. For localised external corrosion without leakage.
2. Clean and prepare surface.
3. Apply composite wrap per manufacturer instructions.
4. Cure time: typically 2–4 hours at ambient temperature.
5. Record repair location and date in asset register.
