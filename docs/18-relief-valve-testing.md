# Relief Valve Testing and Maintenance

## Purpose

Procedures for testing and maintaining Pressure Relief Valves (PRVs) and Safety Relief Valves (SRVs) used in oil refinery process systems.

---

## Safety Warning

- Relief valves are CRITICAL SAFETY DEVICES – never disable or bypass them.
- Testing shall be performed only by trained and authorized personnel.
- Use only test equipment rated for the valve's design pressure.
- Safely vent the discharged process fluid during testing.

---

## Testing Frequency

| Valve Type | Test Interval | Full Overhaul |
|------------|--------------:|--------------:|
| PSV (spring-loaded) | 12 months | 5 years |
| Pilot-operated PRV | 12 months | 5 years |
| Thermal relief valve | 24 months | 5 years |

---

## In-Situ Test Procedure (Bench Test Preferred Where Possible)

1. Confirm bypass or redundant overpressure protection is available.
2. Install a calibrated pressure gauge downstream of the valve.
3. Slowly increase upstream pressure.
4. Record the pressure at which the valve begins to lift (cracking pressure).
5. Record the full-lift pressure (set pressure).
6. Compare the measured pressure with the valve nameplate set pressure.
7. Acceptance criteria:
   - ±3% of set pressure for pressures above 70 bar
   - ±2 bar for pressures below 70 bar
8. Reduce pressure and confirm that the valve reseats completely.
9. Check for leakage after reseating.

---

## Common Faults

| Fault | Symptoms | Corrective Action |
|-------|----------|-------------------|
| Lift pressure too high | Valve does not open at set pressure | Adjust or replace the spring |
| Lift pressure too low | Premature opening, process fluid loss | Adjust or replace the spring |
| Seat leakage | Continuous leakage after reseating | Recondition or replace the seat/disc |
| Chatter | Rapid opening and closing | Verify sizing, backpressure, and piping |
| Valve stuck closed | No response to overpressure | Remove and perform a bench test (possible corrosion) |

---

## Maintenance Procedure

1. Remove the valve (LOTO, isolate, depressurize, and vent).
2. Disassemble and inspect all internal components.
3. Clean the seat and disc; lap if minor damage is present.
4. Replace all soft goods (O-rings, gaskets, diaphragms).
5. Replace the spring if it is out of specification or corroded.
6. Reassemble and perform a bench test to verify the set pressure.
7. Apply a test tag showing the date, technician ID, and next test date.
8. Reinstall the valve and perform a leak test on all connections.
