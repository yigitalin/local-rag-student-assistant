# Emergency Shutdown Procedure (ESD)

## Purpose

Procedure for the safe emergency shutdown of refinery process operations in the event of an uncontrolled hydrocarbon release, fire, or imminent danger.

---

## When to Activate

- Uncontrolled hydrocarbon release that cannot be isolated locally.
- Fire involving refinery process equipment.
- Structural failure of process pipelines or equipment.
- LEL reading exceeding 40% in an occupied area.
- Direction from the control room or emergency response team.

---

## Safety Warnings

- Do **NOT** attempt to extinguish a hydrocarbon fire— isolate the process section instead.
- Evacuate non-essential personnel to the designated assembly point.
- Wear full gas-tested PPE and SCBA when entering hazardous areas.
- Do **NOT** operate electrical equipment in hazardous areas.

---

## Procedure

1. Alert all personnel in the affected area and activate the alarm if available.
2. Evacuate non-essential personnel to the designated assembly point (minimum 100 m upwind).
3. Notify the control room immediately using the designated emergency communication channel.
4. If safe to do so, close the Emergency Shutdown Valve (ESDV).
5. Close upstream and downstream isolation valves.
6. Confirm isolation by monitoring downstream process pressure until it decays to zero.
7. Establish an exclusion zone using barrier tape (minimum 50 m radius).
8. Do **NOT** re-enter the area until authorized by the Refinery Safety Officer.
9. Record all actions taken, personnel involved, and timeline.
10. Complete the incident report within 2 hours.

---

## Post-Incident

- Do not restore process operations until the root cause investigation is complete.
- All affected process pipelines shall be pressure-tested before recommissioning.
- Conduct an HSE review within 24 hours.
