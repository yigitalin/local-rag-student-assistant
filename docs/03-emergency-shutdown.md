# Emergency Shutdown Procedure (ESD)

## Purpose
Procedure for safe emergency shutdown of gas supply in the event of uncontrolled release, fire, or imminent danger.

## When to Activate
- Uncontrolled gas release that cannot be isolated locally.
- Fire involving gas infrastructure.
- Structural failure of pipeline or equipment.
- LEL reading exceeding 40% in occupied area.
- Direction from control room or emergency services.

## Safety Warnings
- Do NOT attempt to extinguish a gas-fed fire – isolate supply instead.
- Evacuate non-essential personnel to upwind assembly point.
- Wear full gas-rated PPE and SCBA if entering hazardous zone.
- Do NOT operate electrical equipment in gas-hazardous zone.

## Procedure
1. Alert all personnel in the area – sound alarm if available.
2. Evacuate non-essential personnel to assembly point (minimum 100m upwind).
3. Notify control room immediately: radio channel 5 or emergency phone.
4. If safe to do so, close Emergency Shutdown Valve (ESDV).
5. Close upstream and downstream isolation valves.
6. Confirm isolation by monitoring downstream pressure – should decay to zero.
7. Mark the exclusion zone with barrier tape (minimum 50m radius).
8. Do NOT re-enter the area until declared safe by Gas Safety Officer.
9. Log all actions taken, personnel involved, and timeline.
10. Complete incident report form IR-001 within 2 hours.

## Post-Incident
- Do not restore supply until root cause investigation is complete.
- All affected pipework must be pressure-tested before recommissioning.
- Review with HSE within 24 hours.
