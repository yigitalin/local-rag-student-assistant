# Purging Procedures – Gas Pipework

## Purpose
Safe procedures for purging gas from pipework (de-gassing) and introducing gas into purged pipework (gassing).

## Safety Warning
- Purging is a HIGH-RISK operation – formal risk assessment required.
- Gas/air mixtures within the flammable range (5–15% methane in air) are EXPLOSIVE.
- Continuous gas monitoring during all purging operations.
- Purging must be supervised by an Authorised Person.
- All personnel briefed on escape routes and emergency procedures.

## Purge Methods

### Direct Purge (Small Systems < 1m³)
- Suitable for small diameter, short length pipework.
- Introduce gas at controlled rate to displace air.
- Monitor at vent until gas concentration reaches >90%.

### Slug Purge (Medium Systems 1–50m³)
- Introduce inert gas slug (nitrogen) between air and natural gas.
- Prevents formation of flammable gas/air mixture.
- Requires pig launcher/receiver or dedicated purge connections.

### Pressure Purge (Large Systems)
- Pressurise with inert gas, then vent.
- Repeat until oxygen content <2%.
- Then introduce natural gas to displace inert.

## De-Gassing Procedure (Gas to Air)
1. Obtain purge permit.
2. Isolate section to be purged.
3. Connect nitrogen supply to inlet.
4. Connect vent to safe discharge point (minimum 3m above ground, away from ignition sources).
5. Begin nitrogen purge at controlled rate.
6. Monitor at vent:
   - Continue until gas reading is 0% LEL.
   - Then continue until oxygen reading is 20.9% (±0.5%).
7. Confirm atmosphere is safe before any hot work.
8. Close purge permit.

## Gassing Procedure (Air to Gas)
1. Obtain purge permit.
2. Confirm pipework has passed tightness test.
3. Connect gas supply.
4. Open vent at far end.
5. Introduce gas at controlled rate.
6. Monitor at vent:
   - Continue until gas reading is >90% and stable.
7. Close vent.
8. Leak-test all connections.
9. Close purge permit.
