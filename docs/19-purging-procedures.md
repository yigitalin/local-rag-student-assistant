# Purging Procedures – Process Piping

## Purpose

Safe procedures for purging process piping before maintenance, inspection, commissioning, or return to service.

---

## Safety Warning

- Purging is a HIGH-RISK operation and requires a formal risk assessment.
- Flammable vapor and air mixtures may create explosive atmospheres.
- Continuous atmospheric monitoring is mandatory throughout the operation.
- Purging shall be supervised by an Authorized Person.
- All personnel shall be briefed on emergency procedures and escape routes.

---

## Purge Methods

### Direct Purge (Small Systems < 1 m³)

- Suitable for small-diameter, short process piping.
- Introduce inert gas at a controlled rate to displace air.
- Monitor oxygen concentration until the required level is achieved.

### Slug Purge (Medium Systems 1–50 m³)

- Introduce an inert gas slug (typically nitrogen) between air and process fluid.
- Prevents formation of flammable vapor/air mixtures.
- Requires dedicated purge connections where applicable.

### Pressure Purge (Large Systems)

- Pressurize using inert gas, then vent.
- Repeat until oxygen concentration is below 2%.
- Confirm the system is ready before introducing the process fluid.

---

## De-Purging Procedure (Process Fluid Removal)

1. Obtain the purge permit.
2. Isolate the process section.
3. Connect the nitrogen supply.
4. Connect the vent line to an approved safe discharge location.
5. Begin nitrogen purging at a controlled flow rate.
6. Monitor the vent:
   - Continue until LEL reaches 0%.
   - Continue until oxygen concentration reaches approximately 20.9%.
7. Verify the atmosphere is safe before beginning maintenance.
8. Close the purge permit.

---

## Recommissioning Procedure (Return to Service)

1. Obtain the purge permit.
2. Verify that the piping has successfully passed pressure or tightness testing.
3. Connect the process fluid supply.
4. Open the vent at the designated discharge point.
5. Introduce the process fluid at a controlled rate.
6. Monitor the vent:
   - Continue until process fluid concentration reaches the required stable level.
7. Close the vent.
8. Perform a leak test on all connections.
9. Close the purge permit.
