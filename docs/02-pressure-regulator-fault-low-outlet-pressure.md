# Pressure Regulator Fault – Low Outlet Pressure

## Symptoms

- Reduced downstream process pressure below setpoint.
- Intermittent or fluctuating process flow.
- Audible hissing from the regulator body.

---

## Possible Causes

1. Blocked inlet filter or strainer.
2. Diaphragm wear, rupture, or hardening.
3. Incorrect regulator setting for current process demand.
4. Upstream process pressure below the minimum operating threshold.
5. Seat damage or debris on the valve seat.

---

## Safety Warning

- Never adjust a pressure regulator under live process pressure.
- Isolate the process line before any disassembly.
- Safely vent trapped pressure before opening the regulator body.
- Ensure the work area is well ventilated.

---

## Diagnostic Steps

1. Isolate the process line upstream and downstream.
2. Verify inlet pressure using a calibrated pressure gauge and confirm it is within the rated operating range.
3. If inlet pressure is normal, proceed with the internal inspection.
4. Remove the inlet filter or strainer and inspect it for blockage.
5. Clean or replace the filter if necessary.
6. Re-test the outlet pressure.
7. If the fault persists, remove the regulator cover and inspect the diaphragm.
8. Replace the diaphragm if signs of wear, cracking, or hardening are present.
9. Inspect the valve seat for scoring or debris. Clean or replace it if required.
10. Reassemble the regulator, restore process pressure, and perform a leak test on all connections.

---

## Torque Values

- Regulator cover bolts: 25–30 Nm
- Inlet/outlet connections: Follow the manufacturer's specification (typically 40–60 Nm)
