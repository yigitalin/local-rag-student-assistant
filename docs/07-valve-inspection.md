# Valve Inspection and Maintenance

## Purpose
Routine inspection and maintenance procedures for gas system valves.

## Safety Warning
- Never operate a valve without confirming downstream conditions.
- Ensure isolation is in place before any valve disassembly.
- Use approved valve keys only – improvised tools are prohibited.

## Valve Types Covered
1. Gate valves
2. Ball valves
3. Plug valves
4. Butterfly valves
5. Emergency shutdown valves (ESDV)

## Inspection Procedure
1. Verify valve tag number against asset register.
2. Visual inspection:
   - Corrosion on body, bonnet, or stem
   - Leakage from gland packing
   - Damage to handwheel or actuator
   - Condition of insulation/protection
3. Operate valve through full travel (open → close → open).
4. Record number of turns and confirm against specification.
5. Check for stiffness or binding.
6. Leak-test gland packing with detection fluid.
7. Inspect flange bolting for corrosion and correct torque.

## Maintenance Actions
### Gland Packing Adjustment
1. Isolate valve (upstream and downstream).
2. Tighten gland nuts ¼ turn at a time.
3. Re-test for leaks.
4. If leak persists after 2 adjustments, replace packing.

### Gland Packing Replacement
1. Isolate and depressurise.
2. Remove gland follower.
3. Extract old packing with packing hook.
4. Install new packing rings (correct material and size).
5. Compress evenly – do not over-tighten.
6. Reassemble, pressurise, and leak-test.

### Torque Values (General)
| Bolt Size | Torque (Nm) |
|-----------|-------------|
| M12 | 60–80 |
| M16 | 120–160 |
| M20 | 200–260 |
| M24 | 350–450 |
