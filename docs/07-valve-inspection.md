# Valve Inspection and Maintenance

## Purpose

Routine inspection and maintenance procedures for refinery process valves.

---

## Safety Warning

- Never operate a valve without confirming downstream process conditions.
- Ensure isolation is in place before any valve disassembly.
- Use approved valve keys only – improvised tools are prohibited.

---

## Valve Types Covered

1. Gate valves
2. Ball valves
3. Plug valves
4. Butterfly valves
5. Emergency Shutdown Valves (ESDV)

---

## Inspection Procedure

1. Verify the valve tag number against the asset register.
2. Perform a visual inspection:
   - Check for corrosion on the valve body, bonnet, and stem.
   - Check for leakage from the gland packing.
   - Inspect the handwheel or actuator for damage.
   - Verify the condition of insulation or protective coating.
3. Operate the valve through its full travel (open → close → open).
4. Record the number of turns and compare with the manufacturer's specification.
5. Check for stiffness or binding during operation.
6. Test the gland packing for leakage using an approved leak detection solution.
7. Inspect flange bolts for corrosion and verify the correct tightening torque.

---

## Maintenance Actions

### Gland Packing Adjustment

1. Isolate the valve (upstream and downstream).
2. Tighten the gland nuts by ¼ turn at a time.
3. Re-test for leakage.
4. If leakage persists after two adjustments, replace the packing.

### Gland Packing Replacement

1. Isolate and depressurize the valve.
2. Remove the gland follower.
3. Extract the old packing using a packing hook.
4. Install new packing rings of the correct material and size.
5. Compress the packing evenly. Do **NOT** over-tighten.
6. Reassemble the valve, restore process pressure, and perform a leak test.
