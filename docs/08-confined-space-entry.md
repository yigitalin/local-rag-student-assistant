# Confined Space Entry – Oil Refinery Operations

## Purpose

Safe entry procedures for confined spaces within oil refinery operations.

---

## Safety Warning

- Confined space entry is a PERMIT-REQUIRED activity.
- NEVER enter a confined space alone.
- Atmospheric monitoring is MANDATORY before and during entry.
- A rescue plan must be in place BEFORE entry.

---

## Definition

A confined space is any enclosed or partially enclosed area with:

- Limited entry/exit
- Not designed for continuous occupancy
- Risk of hazardous atmosphere, engulfment, or entrapment

---

## Examples in Oil Refinery Operations

- Process vessels
- Storage tanks
- Reactor vessels
- Pipeline pits
- Valve chambers
- Distillation columns

---

## Pre-Entry Requirements

1. Confined Space Entry Permit (CSEP) completed and signed by an authorized person.
2. Risk assessment and method statement reviewed.
3. Atmospheric testing performed:
   - Oxygen: 19.5%–23.5%
   - LEL: <10%
   - H₂S: <1 ppm
   - CO: <25 ppm
4. Mechanical ventilation established where required.
5. Rescue equipment positioned at the entry point:
   - Tripod and winch
   - Rescue harness on entrant
   - Communication system tested
6. Standby person stationed at the entry point and MUST NOT LEAVE.

---

## Entry Procedure

1. Confirm the permit is valid and displayed at the entry point.
2. Entrant wears a rescue harness connected to the retrieval system.
3. Continuously monitor the atmosphere using a calibrated hydrocarbon gas detector.
4. Maintain communication with the standby person at least every 5 minutes.
5. If any alarm activates, EVACUATE IMMEDIATELY.
6. Follow the maximum entry duration specified on the permit.
7. On exit, confirm that all personnel are accounted for.
8. Close the permit and record completion.

---

## Emergency Rescue

- The standby person activates the rescue winch. DO NOT ENTER the confined space.
- Notify the emergency response team immediately.
- Continue ventilation during the rescue operation if safe to do so.
