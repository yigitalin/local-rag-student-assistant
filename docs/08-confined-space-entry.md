# Confined Space Entry – Gas Installations

## Purpose
Safe entry procedures for confined spaces within gas installations.

## Safety Warning
- Confined space entry is a PERMIT-REQUIRED activity.
- NEVER enter a confined space alone.
- Atmospheric monitoring is MANDATORY before and during entry.
- Rescue plan must be in place BEFORE entry.

## Definition
A confined space is any enclosed or partially enclosed area with:
- Limited entry/exit
- Not designed for continuous occupancy
- Risk of hazardous atmosphere, engulfment, or entrapment

## Examples in Gas Installations
- Below-ground valve chambers
- Pipeline pits
- Meter housings (large)
- Tank interiors
- Vent stacks

## Pre-Entry Requirements
1. Confined Space Entry Permit (CSEP) completed and signed by Authorised Person.
2. Risk assessment and method statement reviewed.
3. Atmospheric testing performed:
   - Oxygen: 19.5% – 23.5%
   - LEL: <10%
   - H₂S: <1 ppm
   - CO: <25 ppm
4. Ventilation established (mechanical preferred).
5. Rescue equipment staged at entry point:
   - Tripod and winch
   - Rescue harness on entrant
   - Communication system tested
6. Standby person stationed at entry – MUST NOT LEAVE.

## Entry Procedure
1. Confirm permit is live and displayed at entry.
2. Entrant dons harness attached to rescue winch.
3. Continuous gas monitoring on entrant's person.
4. Maintain communication with standby every 5 minutes.
5. If any alarm sounds: EVACUATE IMMEDIATELY.
6. Maximum entry duration: as specified on permit (typically 1 hour).
7. On exit, confirm all personnel accounted for.
8. Close permit and record completion.

## Emergency Rescue
- Standby person activates rescue winch – DO NOT ENTER to rescue.
- Call emergency services immediately.
- Ventilate space continuously during rescue.
