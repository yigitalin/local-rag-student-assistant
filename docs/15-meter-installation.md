# Flow Meter Installation and Commissioning

## Purpose

Procedures for installing and commissioning flow meters used in oil refinery process systems.

---

## Safety Warning

- Installation work may involve live process lines – continuous atmospheric monitoring is required where applicable.
- Ensure adequate ventilation at the work location.
- Perform a leak test on all connections after installation.

---

## Pre-Installation Checks

1. Verify that the flow meter type and size match the design specification.
2. Confirm that the calibration certificate is valid.
3. Inspect the flow meter for transport damage.
4. Verify that the inlet and outlet orientation matches the process piping.
5. Confirm isolation valves are installed upstream and downstream.

---

## Installation Procedure

1. Close the upstream isolation valve.
2. Depressurize the downstream process piping safely.
3. Verify zero pressure.
4. Remove blanking flanges from the piping.
5. Apply the approved thread sealant or install the correct gaskets.
6. Install the flow meter with the flow arrow in the correct direction.
7. Connect the inlet piping and tighten according to specification.
8. Connect the outlet piping and tighten according to specification.
9. Support the flow meter adequately without imposing stress on the piping.

---

## Commissioning Procedure

1. Slowly open the upstream isolation valve (¼ turn, wait 30 seconds).
2. Fully open the valve after confirming no leakage.
3. Open the downstream isolation valve.
4. Perform a leak test on all connections using an approved leak detection solution.
5. Verify that the flow meter registers flow correctly.
6. Record the serial number, initial reading, and installation date.
7. Zero the totalizer if required for a new installation.
8. Apply tamper seals where required.
9. Complete the Flow Meter Installation Certificate (MIC-001).

---

## Post-Commissioning

- Verify telemetry or communication output (if equipped).
- Confirm that the flow meter data is available in the plant asset management system.
- Update the P&ID and maintenance records.
