# Gas Meter Installation and Commissioning

## Purpose
Procedures for installing and commissioning gas meters at field installations.

## Safety Warning
- Work on gas meters involves live gas – continuous gas monitoring required.
- Ensure adequate ventilation at meter location.
- Test for gas tightness at ALL connections after installation.

## Pre-Installation Checks
1. Verify meter type and size match design specification.
2. Check meter calibration certificate is valid.
3. Inspect meter for transit damage.
4. Confirm inlet/outlet orientation matches pipework.
5. Ensure isolation valves are present upstream and downstream.

## Installation Procedure
1. Close upstream isolation valve.
2. Vent downstream pipework safely.
3. Confirm zero pressure.
4. Remove blanking flanges from pipework connections.
5. Apply approved thread sealant or install correct gaskets.
6. Position meter with flow arrow matching gas direction.
7. Connect inlet pipework – hand-tight then wrench-tighten.
8. Connect outlet pipework – hand-tight then wrench-tighten.
9. Support meter adequately – do NOT stress pipework connections.

## Commissioning Procedure
1. Slowly open upstream isolation valve (¼ turn, wait 30 seconds).
2. Open fully once no leaks detected at inlet.
3. Open downstream isolation valve.
4. Leak-test ALL connections with approved detection fluid.
5. Verify meter registers flow correctly:
   - Open downstream appliance or test port.
   - Confirm meter display increments.
6. Record meter serial number, reading, and installation date.
7. Set meter index to zero if new installation.
8. Apply tamper seal to meter connections.
9. Complete Meter Installation Certificate (MIC-001).

## Post-Commissioning
- Verify telemetry connection (if fitted) is transmitting.
- Confirm meter data appears in local asset database.
- Update site drawings.
