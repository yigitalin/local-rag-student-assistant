# Gas Sensor Calibration Procedures

## Purpose
Calibration procedures for portable and fixed gas detection sensors used in field operations.

## Safety Warning
- Calibration must be performed in a clean, well-ventilated area.
- Use only certified calibration gases within their expiry date.
- A sensor that fails calibration is UNFIT for use – remove from service immediately.

## Calibration Types

### Bump Test (Pre-Shift)
- Quick functional check exposing sensor to known gas concentration.
- Pass criteria: sensor reads within ±10% of applied concentration.
- Duration: 30–60 seconds.
- Required: before every shift.

### Full Span Calibration
- Multi-point calibration against certified reference gases.
- Required: every 30 days minimum, or after any bump test failure.

## Bump Test Procedure
1. Power on instrument in clean air.
2. Wait for warm-up and zero reading.
3. Connect calibration gas adapter.
4. Apply bump test gas at specified flow rate (0.5–1.0 L/min).
5. Observe sensor response:
   - If within ±10% of target → PASS
   - If outside ±10% → FAIL – proceed to full calibration
6. Record result in calibration log.

## Full Calibration Procedure
1. Power on instrument in clean air.
2. Perform zero calibration (fresh air zero).
3. Connect span gas for first sensor channel.
4. Apply gas at specified flow rate until reading stabilises.
5. Confirm instrument adjusts to match applied concentration.
6. Repeat for each sensor channel (LEL, O₂, CO, H₂S).
7. Record all readings and gas cylinder lot numbers.
8. Apply calibration sticker with date and technician ID.

## Calibration Gas Specifications
| Gas | Concentration | Cylinder Life |
|-----|---------------|---------------|
| CH₄ (methane) | 2.5% vol (50% LEL) | 24 months |
| O₂ | 18.0% vol | 24 months |
| CO | 100 ppm | 24 months |
| H₂S | 25 ppm | 12 months |
