# Hydrocarbon Leak Detection – Field Procedure

## Purpose

To identify and confirm hydrocarbon leaks during routine or reactive inspections within the oil refinery.

---

## Safety Warnings

- Ensure all ignition sources are eliminated before beginning leak detection.
- Wear approved flame-resistant clothing, safety boots, hard hat, safety goggles, gloves, and a calibrated hydrocarbon gas detector.
- Maintain a minimum safe distance of 3 meters during the initial detection sweep.
- If the LEL reading exceeds 20%, evacuate the area immediately and notify the control room and emergency response team.

---

## Equipment Required

- Portable hydrocarbon gas detector (calibrated within the last 30 days)
- Leak detection fluid (approved type)
- Isolation tools
- Communication radio

---

## Procedure

1. Perform a visual inspection of all pipelines, fittings, flanges, valves, pump seals, and connection points.
2. Check the gas detector calibration date. Do **NOT** use it if expired.
3. Power on the gas detector and confirm a zero reading in clean air.
4. Move the detector slowly (2–3 cm/s) around joints, flanges, valve stems, pump seals, and pipe connections.
5. If hydrocarbon vapor is detected above the threshold (>1% LEL), stop work immediately.
6. Mark the suspected leak location and isolate the affected section using approved refinery isolation procedures.
7. Notify the control room and follow the refinery emergency response procedure.
8. If below the threshold, apply leak detection fluid to suspected points.
9. Observe for bubble formation for a minimum of 30 seconds.
10. Record all findings in the refinery inspection log.

---

## Post-Detection

- Report all confirmed leaks to the control room within 15 minutes.
- Do **NOT** attempt repairs until isolation is confirmed and work authorization is approved.
