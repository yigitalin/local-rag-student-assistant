# Hot Work Permit Procedure

## Purpose
Procedure for obtaining and managing hot work permits for gas installations where welding, cutting, grinding, or other ignition-producing activities are required.

## Safety Warning
- Hot work near gas installations is EXTREMELY HIGH RISK.
- An active hot work permit is MANDATORY before any hot work begins.
- Continuous gas monitoring is required throughout.
- A fire watch must remain for minimum 60 minutes after work completion.

## Definition of Hot Work
Any activity producing sparks, flames, or heat sufficient to ignite gas:
- Welding (arc, MIG, TIG, oxy-fuel)
- Cutting (oxy-fuel, plasma, abrasive disc)
- Grinding
- Brazing and soldering
- Use of non-intrinsically safe electrical equipment in hazardous zones

## Permit Application
1. Complete Hot Work Permit Application (HWPA-001).
2. Submit to Authorised Person (AP) minimum 24 hours before planned work.
3. AP conducts site survey and risk assessment.
4. AP issues permit specifying:
   - Exact work location
   - Duration (maximum 12 hours per permit)
   - Required precautions
   - Gas monitoring requirements
   - Fire watch requirements

## Pre-Work Checks
1. Confirm pipeline/equipment is isolated, purged, and gas-free.
2. Atmospheric test: LEL must be 0% at work location.
3. Remove or protect combustible materials within 10m radius.
4. Fire extinguishers staged within 5m of work area.
5. Fire watch person briefed and in position.
6. All workers briefed on permit conditions.

## During Hot Work
1. Continuous gas monitoring – stop if LEL >0%.
2. Fire watch maintains observation.
3. Permit must be displayed at work location.
4. No deviation from permitted scope without re-assessment.

## After Hot Work
1. Fire watch continues for minimum 60 minutes.
2. Final gas and temperature survey of area.
3. Fire watch signs off on permit.
4. Return permit to AP for closure.
