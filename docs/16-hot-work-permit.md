# Hot Work Permit Procedure

## Purpose

Procedure for obtaining and managing hot work permits in oil refinery operations where welding, cutting, grinding, or other ignition-producing activities are required.

---

## Safety Warning

- Hot work in refinery process areas is EXTREMELY HIGH RISK.
- An approved Hot Work Permit is REQUIRED before any hot work begins.
- Continuous atmospheric monitoring is required throughout the work.
- A fire watch must remain in place for a minimum of 60 minutes after work completion.

---

## Definition of Hot Work

Any activity capable of producing sparks, flames, or sufficient heat to ignite flammable materials or vapors, including:

- Welding (arc, MIG, TIG, oxy-fuel)
- Cutting (oxy-fuel, plasma, abrasive disc)
- Grinding
- Brazing and soldering
- Use of non-intrinsically safe electrical equipment in hazardous areas

---

## Permit Application

1. Complete the Hot Work Permit Application (HWPA-001).
2. Submit the application to the Authorized Person (AP) at least 24 hours before the planned work.
3. The AP conducts a site survey and risk assessment.
4. The AP issues the permit specifying:
   - Exact work location
   - Permit duration (maximum 12 hours)
   - Required precautions
   - Atmospheric monitoring requirements
   - Fire watch requirements

---

## Pre-Work Checks

1. Confirm that the process line or equipment is isolated, depressurized, and gas-free.
2. Verify atmospheric test results (LEL = 0% at the work location).
3. Remove or protect combustible materials within a 10 m radius.
4. Position fire extinguishers within 5 m of the work area.
5. Ensure the fire watch is briefed and in position.
6. Brief all personnel on the permit conditions.

---

## During Hot Work

1. Maintain continuous atmospheric monitoring. Stop work immediately if LEL exceeds 0%.
2. The fire watch maintains continuous observation.
3. Display the permit at the work location.
4. Do not deviate from the approved work scope without re-assessment.

---

## After Hot Work

1. Maintain the fire watch for a minimum of 60 minutes.
2. Perform a final atmospheric and temperature survey of the area.
3. Fire watch signs off the permit.
4. Return the permit to the Authorized Person (AP) for closure.
