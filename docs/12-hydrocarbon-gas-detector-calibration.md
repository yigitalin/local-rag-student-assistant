# Hydrocarbon Gas Detector Calibration Procedures

## Purpose

Calibration procedures for portable and fixed hydrocarbon gas detection sensors used in oil refinery operations.

---

## Safety Warning

- Perform calibration in a clean, well-ventilated area.
- Use only certified calibration gases within their expiration date.
- Any detector that fails calibration is UNFIT for use and must be removed from service immediately.

---

## Calibration Types

### Bump Test (Pre-Shift)

- Quick functional check by exposing the detector to a known gas concentration.
- Pass criteria: Detector reading within ±10% of the applied concentration.
- Duration: 30–60 seconds.
- Required before every shift.

### Full Span Calibration

- Multi-point calibration using certified reference gases.
- Required at least every 30 days or after any failed bump test.

---

## Bump Test Procedure

1. Power on the detector in clean air.
2. Wait until warm-up is complete and the zero reading is stable.
3. Connect the calibration gas adapter.
4. Apply calibration gas at the specified flow rate (0.5–1.0 L/min).
5. Observe the detector response:
   - Within ±10% of the target value → PASS
   - Outside ±10% → FAIL – proceed to full calibration
6. Record the result in the calibration log.

---

## Full Calibration Procedure

1. Power on the detector in clean air.
2. Perform a fresh-air zero calibration.
3. Connect the span gas for the first sensor channel.
4. Apply gas at the specified flow rate until the reading stabilizes.
5. Verify that the detector adjusts to the applied concentration.
6. Repeat for each sensor channel (LEL, O₂, CO, H₂S).
7. Record all readings and calibration gas cylinder batch numbers.
8. Apply a calibration label showing the date and technician ID.

---

## Calibration Gas Specifications

| Gas | Concentration | Cylinder Life |
|-----|--------------:|--------------:|
| CH₄ (Methane) | 2.5% vol (50% LEL) | 24 months |
| O₂ | 18.0% vol | 24 months |
| CO | 100 ppm | 24 months |
| H₂S | 25 ppm | 12 months |
