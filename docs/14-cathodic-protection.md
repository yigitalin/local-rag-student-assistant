# Cathodic Protection System Maintenance

## Purpose

Maintenance procedures for impressed current and sacrificial anode cathodic protection (CP) systems used to protect refinery process pipelines and buried assets.

---

## Safety Warning

- Impressed current systems operate at potentially lethal voltages.
- Apply Lock Out / Tag Out (LOTO) to the rectifier before performing electrical work.
- Do NOT disconnect CP bonds on energized systems without authorization.

---

## Routine Inspections

### Monthly – Test Post Readings

1. Measure pipe-to-soil potential at each test post.
2. Use a Cu/CuSO₄ reference electrode positioned directly above the pipeline.
3. Record ON and OFF potentials (instant-off within 1 second of interruption).
4. Acceptable criteria:
   - ON potential: More negative than -850 mV
   - OFF potential: More negative than -850 mV
   - Do not exceed -1200 mV (over-protection risk)

---

### Quarterly – Rectifier Check

1. Record rectifier output voltage and current.
2. Compare measured values with the design output.
3. Check for overheating, corrosion, or loose electrical connections.
4. Verify that tap settings match the design specifications.
5. Inspect DC cables and connections.
6. Verify AC power supply breaker operation.

---

### Annual – Full Survey

1. Perform a Close Interval Potential Survey (CIPS).
2. Perform a DCVG coating defect survey.
3. Measure anode bed resistance.
4. Test rectifier efficiency.
5. Inspect interference bond currents.
6. Update CP maintenance records and trend analysis.

---

## Common Issues and Corrective Actions

| Issue | Possible Cause | Corrective Action |
|------|----------------|------------------|
| Potential less negative than -850 mV | Anode depletion or coating damage | Investigate, increase rectifier output, replace anodes if required |
| Potential more negative than -1200 mV | Over-protection | Reduce rectifier output |
| Rectifier output failure | AC supply failure or blown fuse | Check power supply and replace fuse |
| Fluctuating readings | Stray current interference | Install drainage bond and investigate the interference source |
