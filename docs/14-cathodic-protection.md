# Cathodic Protection System Maintenance

## Purpose
Maintenance procedures for impressed current and sacrificial anode cathodic protection (CP) systems on buried gas pipelines.

## Safety Warning
- Impressed current systems operate at potentially lethal voltages.
- Lock out / tag out rectifier before any electrical work.
- Do NOT disconnect CP bonds on live systems without authorisation.

## Routine Inspections

### Monthly – Test Post Readings
1. Measure pipe-to-soil potential at each test post.
2. Use Cu/CuSO₄ reference electrode, placed on soil directly above pipe.
3. Record ON and OFF potentials (instant-off within 1 second of interruption).
4. Acceptable criteria:
   - ON potential: more negative than -850 mV
   - OFF potential: more negative than -850 mV
   - No reading more negative than -1200 mV (over-protection risk)

### Quarterly – Rectifier Check
1. Record output voltage and current.
2. Compare with design output.
3. Check for overheating, corrosion, or loose connections.
4. Verify tap settings match design.
5. Inspect DC cabling and connections.
6. Check AC supply breaker operation.

### Annual – Full Survey
1. Close Interval Potential Survey (CIPS).
2. DCVG coating defect survey.
3. Anode bed resistance measurement.
4. Rectifier efficiency test.
5. Interference bond current checks.
6. Update CP records and trend analysis.

## Common Issues and Actions
| Issue | Possible Cause | Action |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| Potential less negative than -850 mV | Anode depletion, coating damage | Investigate, boost rectifier, replace anodes |
| Potential more negative than -1200 mV | Over-protection | Reduce rectifier output |
| Rectifier no output | AC supply failure, fuse blown | Check supply, replace fuse |
| Fluctuating readings | Stray current interference | Install drainage bond, investigate source |
