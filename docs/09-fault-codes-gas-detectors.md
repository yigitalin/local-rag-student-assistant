# Common Fault Codes – Gas Detection Equipment

## Purpose
Quick reference for fault codes displayed by portable gas detection equipment commonly used in gas field operations.

## Safety Warning
- A faulty gas detector means you have NO atmospheric protection.
- Do NOT enter hazardous areas with a malfunctioning detector.
- Replace or repair immediately – do not attempt field-level sensor repair.

## Fault Code Reference

### Code: E01 – Sensor Failure
- **Symptom:** "SENSOR FAIL" or "E01" on display
- **Cause:** Sensor exhausted, poisoned, or disconnected
- **Action:**
  1. Power off unit.
  2. Remove and reseat sensor.
  3. Power on and re-test.
  4. If fault persists, replace sensor.
  5. Send unit for recalibration.

### Code: E02 – Calibration Overdue
- **Symptom:** "CAL DUE" warning
- **Cause:** Calibration interval exceeded (>30 days)
- **Action:**
  1. Do NOT use for safety-critical monitoring.
  2. Return unit to calibration station.
  3. Perform full span calibration.
  4. Record new calibration date.

### Code: E03 – Low Battery
- **Symptom:** Battery icon flashing, "LOW BATT"
- **Cause:** Insufficient charge
- **Action:**
  1. Replace battery or charge unit.
  2. Do NOT continue monitoring on low battery.
  3. If in hazardous area, evacuate and swap unit.

### Code: E04 – Over-Range
- **Symptom:** Display shows "OL" or ">100% LEL"
- **Cause:** Gas concentration exceeds sensor range
- **Action:**
  1. EVACUATE IMMEDIATELY.
  2. Notify control room.
  3. Do NOT re-enter until area is ventilated and confirmed safe.

### Code: E05 – Bump Test Fail
- **Symptom:** "BUMP FAIL" after bump test
- **Cause:** Sensor response outside acceptable range
- **Action:**
  1. Attempt bump test again with fresh calibration gas.
  2. If still fails, send for full calibration.
  3. Use alternative detector for shift.

### Code: E06 – Pump Fault
- **Symptom:** "PUMP ERROR" – applies to pump-draw models
- **Cause:** Blocked inlet, pump diaphragm failure
- **Action:**
  1. Check inlet for obstruction.
  2. Replace inline filter.
  3. If fault persists, send for repair.
