# Gasket Replacement Procedure – Flanged Joints

## Purpose
Step-by-step procedure for replacing gaskets on flanged joints in gas pipework systems.

## Safety Warnings
- Isolate and depressurise the joint BEFORE disassembly.
- Confirm zero energy using calibrated pressure gauge.
- Vent residual gas safely to atmosphere (downwind, away from ignition sources).
- New gaskets must be correct material and rating – verify BEFORE installation.

## Materials
- Gasket: spiral wound with graphite filler (ASME B16.20) or as per design spec
- Bolt lubricant: approved anti-seize compound
- Flange protector tape (temporary)

## Tools Required
- Calibrated torque wrench
- Flange spreader
- Wire brush (non-sparking, brass)
- Gasket scraper (non-sparking)
- Alignment pins

## Procedure
1. Isolate section – close upstream and downstream valves.
2. Depressurise and vent to atmosphere.
3. Confirm zero pressure on gauge.
4. Loosen bolts in cross-pattern sequence (¼ turn at a time).
5. Remove bolts and set aside (inspect for damage).
6. Use flange spreader to separate flanges – do NOT pry with screwdriver.
7. Remove old gasket completely – do NOT leave fragments.
8. Clean flange faces with brass wire brush and scraper.
9. Inspect flange faces for scoring, pitting, or corrosion.
   - If damage exceeds 1mm depth, report for engineering assessment.
10. Place new gasket centrally using alignment pins.
11. Hand-tighten bolts in cross-pattern sequence.
12. Torque bolts in 3 passes (cross-pattern):
    - Pass 1: 30% of final torque
    - Pass 2: 60% of final torque
    - Pass 3: 100% of final torque
13. Pressurise and perform tightness test per DOC-IP-003.
14. Leak-test all joints with detection fluid.

## Torque Values (Spiral Wound Gaskets, Class 150)
| Bolt Size | Torque (Nm) |
|-----------|-------------|
| 5/8" | 95 |
| 3/4" | 170 |
| 7/8" | 265 |
| 1" | 400 |
