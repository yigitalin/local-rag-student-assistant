# Gasket Replacement Procedure – Flanged Joints

## Purpose

Step-by-step procedure for replacing gaskets on flanged joints in refinery process piping systems.

---

## Safety Warnings

- Isolate and depressurize the joint BEFORE disassembly.
- Confirm zero pressure using a calibrated pressure gauge.
- Vent residual process pressure safely before opening the joint.
- Verify that the replacement gasket matches the required material specification and pressure rating.

---

## Materials

- Spiral wound gasket with graphite filler (ASME B16.20) or equivalent design specification
- Approved anti-seize compound for flange bolts
- Temporary flange protection covers

---

## Tools Required

- Calibrated torque wrench
- Flange spreader
- Non-sparking brass wire brush
- Non-sparking gasket scraper
- Alignment pins

---

## Procedure

1. Isolate the process section by closing upstream and downstream valves.
2. Depressurize the joint and vent residual process pressure safely.
3. Verify zero pressure using a calibrated pressure gauge.
4. Loosen bolts using a cross-pattern sequence (¼ turn at a time).
5. Remove bolts and inspect them for damage.
6. Separate the flanges using a flange spreader. Do NOT pry with a screwdriver.
7. Remove the old gasket completely. Ensure no gasket fragments remain.
8. Clean the flange faces using a brass wire brush and gasket scraper.
9. Inspect the flange faces for scoring, pitting, or corrosion.
   - If damage exceeds 1 mm in depth, report it for engineering assessment.
10. Position the new gasket centrally using alignment pins.
11. Hand-tighten the bolts using a cross-pattern sequence.
12. Tighten the bolts in three torque passes:
    - Pass 1: 30% of final torque
    - Pass 2: 60% of final torque
    - Pass 3: 100% of final torque
13. Restore process pressure and perform a tightness test according to DOC-IP-003.
14. Perform a leak test on all flange joints using an approved leak detection solution.

---

## Torque Values (Spiral Wound Gaskets, Class 150)

| Bolt Size | Torque (Nm) |
|-----------|------------:|
| 5/8" | 95 |
| 3/4" | 170 |
| 7/8" | 265 |
| 1" | 400 |
