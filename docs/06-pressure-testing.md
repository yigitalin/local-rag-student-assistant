# Pressure Testing Procedures

## Purpose
Procedures for strength and tightness testing of gas pipework and equipment.

## Safety Warnings
- Pressure testing is a HIGH-RISK activity – risk assessment mandatory.
- Clear the blast zone before pressurising.
- Use calibrated gauges only.
- Never exceed maximum allowable test pressure.
- Wear face shield and body protection during strength tests.

## Types of Pressure Test

### 1. Strength Test (Pneumatic)
- **Medium:** Air or nitrogen (NEVER use gas)
- **Pressure:** 1.5 × Maximum Operating Pressure (MOP)
- **Duration:** Minimum 1 hour for pipework <1 inch; 4 hours for larger
- **Acceptance:** No pressure drop

### 2. Tightness Test
- **Medium:** Air or nitrogen
- **Pressure:** Operating pressure
- **Duration:** Per pipework volume (see table below)
- **Acceptance:** No measurable pressure drop

### Tightness Test Duration Table
| Pipe Volume | Minimum Duration |
|-------------|-----------------|
| < 0.01 m³ | 1 minute |
| 0.01 – 0.1 m³ | 5 minutes |
| 0.1 – 1.0 m³ | 30 minutes |
| > 1.0 m³ | See IGE/UP/1A |

## Procedure
1. Isolate the section to be tested.
2. Connect calibrated test gauge (accuracy ±0.1 bar).
3. Pressurise slowly to test pressure.
4. Allow temperature stabilisation (minimum 15 minutes for strength test).
5. Begin timed test period.
6. Record start pressure, temperature, and time.
7. Record end pressure, temperature, and time.
8. Calculate any pressure drop (correct for temperature if necessary).
9. If test fails:
   a. Depressurise safely.
   b. Investigate and repair.
   c. Re-test from step 1.
10. Record results on Pressure Test Certificate (PTC-001).
