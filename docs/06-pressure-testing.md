# Pressure Testing Procedures

## Purpose

Procedures for strength and tightness testing of refinery process piping and equipment.

---

## Safety Warnings

- Pressure testing is a HIGH-RISK activity – risk assessment is mandatory.
- Clear the blast zone before pressurizing.
- Use calibrated pressure gauges only.
- Never exceed the maximum allowable test pressure.
- Wear face shield and appropriate body protection during strength tests.

---

## Types of Pressure Test

### 1. Strength Test (Pneumatic)

- **Medium:** Air or nitrogen (NEVER use hydrocarbon gas)
- **Pressure:** 1.5 × Maximum Operating Pressure (MOP)
- **Duration:** Minimum 1 hour for piping smaller than 1 inch; 4 hours for larger piping
- **Acceptance:** No pressure drop

### 2. Tightness Test

- **Medium:** Air or nitrogen
- **Pressure:** Operating pressure
- **Duration:** According to piping volume (see table below)
- **Acceptance:** No measurable pressure drop

---

## Tightness Test Duration Table

| Pipe Volume | Minimum Duration |
|-------------|------------------|
| < 0.01 m³ | 1 minute |
| 0.01–0.1 m³ | 5 minutes |
| 0.1–1.0 m³ | 30 minutes |
| > 1.0 m³ | See refinery pressure testing standard |

---

## Procedure

1. Isolate the process section to be tested.
2. Connect a calibrated pressure gauge (accuracy ±0.1 bar).
3. Pressurize the system slowly to the specified test pressure.
4. Allow temperature stabilization (minimum 15 minutes for strength testing).
5. Begin the timed test period.
6. Record the initial pressure, temperature, and time.
7. Record the final pressure, temperature, and time.
8. Calculate any pressure drop and compensate for temperature changes if required.
9. If the test fails:
   - Depressurize the system safely.
   - Investigate and repair the fault.
   - Repeat the test from Step 1.
10. Record the results on the Pressure Test Certificate (PTC-001).
