# Pipeline Integrity Inspection

## Purpose

Procedures for assessing the structural integrity of refinery process pipelines.

---

## Safety Warning

- Pipeline inspection may require working near hazardous process areas – continuous atmospheric monitoring is mandatory.
- Do NOT excavate near underground pipelines without confirming their location (CAT scan + signal generator).
- Maintain the required minimum clearance from live process lines.

---

## Inspection Types

### 1. Visual Inspection (External)

- Coating condition (disbondment, cracking, holidays)
- Corrosion (surface rust, pitting, wall loss)
- Mechanical damage (dents, gouges, scrapes)
- Ground movement indicators (soil cracking, subsidence)
- Third-party activity near the pipeline corridor

### 2. Coating Survey

- DCVG (Direct Current Voltage Gradient) survey for coating defects
- CIPS (Close Interval Potential Survey) for cathodic protection levels

### 3. Wall Thickness Measurement

- Ultrasonic thickness testing (UT) at predetermined inspection points
- Record readings and compare with previous inspection reports
- Minimum wall thickness according to API 5L or design specification
- Flag any reading below 87.5% of nominal wall thickness

### 4. Cathodic Protection Check

- Measure pipe-to-soil potential at test posts
- Acceptable range: -850 mV to -1200 mV (Cu/CuSO₄ reference)
- Record rectifier output (voltage and current)
- Inspect anode bed condition if protection is inadequate

---

## Procedure

1. Obtain the work permit and confirm the pipeline location.
2. Establish continuous atmospheric monitoring in the work area.
3. If excavation is required, hand-dig within 500 mm of the pipeline.
4. Clean the pipe surface at the inspection points.
5. Perform a visual inspection and photograph all findings.
6. Conduct ultrasonic wall thickness measurements.
7. Measure the pipe-to-soil potential.
8. Record all inspection data in the Pipeline Inspection Report (PIR-001).
9. Reinstate the excavation area after inspection.

---

## Defect Classification

| Category | Description | Response Time |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Priority 1 | Active leak or wall loss >40% | Immediate action |
| Priority 2 | Significant coating defect or inadequate cathodic protection | Within 7 days |
| Priority 3 | Minor coating damage (cosmetic) | Next planned maintenance |
