# Gas Compressor Maintenance

## Purpose
Routine maintenance procedures for reciprocating and screw-type gas compressors used in gas field operations.

## Safety Warnings
- Lock out / tag out (LOTO) before any maintenance.
- Vent residual pressure before opening any connection.
- Compressor surfaces may be hot – allow cooling time.
- Ensure adequate ventilation in compressor enclosures.

## Daily Checks
1. Check oil level and condition (visual).
2. Inspect for gas/oil leaks around connections.
3. Verify operating pressures within normal range.
4. Check vibration levels (abnormal vibration = stop and investigate).
5. Confirm cooling system operation (fan, coolant level).
6. Review control panel for fault alarms.

## Weekly Maintenance
1. Drain condensate from receivers and separators.
2. Check and clean air intake filters.
3. Inspect drive belts for wear and tension.
4. Verify safety valve operation (visual check only – do NOT manually lift).
5. Record operating hours.

## Monthly Maintenance
1. Oil sampling and analysis.
2. Check valve clearances (reciprocating type).
3. Inspect packing glands for leakage.
4. Test auto-shutdown systems.
5. Clean or replace oil filters.

## Annual Maintenance
1. Full valve overhaul (suction and discharge).
2. Replace piston rings and rider rings (reciprocating).
3. Inspect rotor condition (screw type).
4. Pressure test all safety valves.
5. Full alignment check.
6. Review and update maintenance plan.

## Common Fault Codes
| Code | Description | Action |
|------|-------------|--------|
| C-01 | High discharge temperature | Check cooling system, clean cooler |
| C-02 | Low oil pressure | Check oil level, inspect oil pump |
| C-03 | High vibration | Check alignment, inspect bearings |
| C-04 | Overload trip | Check gas conditions, inspect valves |
| C-05 | Seal gas pressure low | Inspect seal system, check filters |
