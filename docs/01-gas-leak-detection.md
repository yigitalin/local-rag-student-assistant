# Gas Leak Detection – Field Procedure

## Purpose
To identify and confirm gas leaks during routine or reactive field inspections.

## Safety Warnings
- Ensure ALL ignition sources are eliminated before beginning detection.
- Wear approved gas-rated PPE including flame-resistant clothing, safety boots, hard hat, and gas monitor.
- Maintain minimum safe distance of 3 metres during initial detection sweep.
- If LEL reading exceeds 20%, evacuate area immediately and call emergency control.

## Equipment Required
- Portable gas detector (calibrated within last 30 days)
- Leak detection fluid (approved type)
- Isolation tools
- Communication radio

## Procedure
1. Perform visual inspection of all joints, fittings, and connection points.
2. Check gas detector calibration date – do NOT use if expired.
3. Power on gas detector and confirm zero reading in clean air.
4. Move detector slowly (2–3 cm/s) around joints, flanges, and valve glands.
5. If gas is detected above threshold (>1% LEL):
   a. Stop work immediately.
   b. Mark the leak location.
   c. Isolate the section using upstream and downstream valves.
   d. Follow emergency protocol GP-EM-01.
6. If below threshold, apply leak detection fluid to suspect joints.
7. Observe for bubble formation (minimum 30 seconds).
8. Record all findings in field inspection log.

## Post-Detection
- Report all confirmed leaks to control room within 15 minutes.
- Do NOT attempt repair until isolation is confirmed and authorised.
