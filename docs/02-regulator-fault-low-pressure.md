# Pressure Regulator Fault – Low Outlet Pressure

## Symptoms
- Reduced downstream pressure below setpoint.
- Intermittent or fluctuating flow.
- Audible hissing from regulator body.

## Possible Causes
1. Blocked inlet filter or strainer.
2. Diaphragm wear, perforation, or hardening.
3. Incorrect regulator sizing for current demand.
4. Upstream supply pressure below minimum operating threshold.
5. Seat damage or debris on seat.

## Safety Warning
- **Never adjust a regulator under live pressure.**
- Isolate gas supply before any disassembly.
- Vent trapped gas safely before opening regulator body.
- Ensure area is well-ventilated.

## Diagnostic Steps
1. Isolate gas supply upstream and downstream.
2. Verify inlet pressure using calibrated gauge – confirm within rated range.
3. If inlet pressure is normal, proceed to internal inspection.
4. Remove inlet filter/strainer and inspect for blockage.
5. Clean or replace filter as required.
6. Re-test outlet pressure.
7. If fault persists, remove regulator cover and inspect diaphragm.
8. Replace diaphragm if any signs of wear, cracking, or hardening.
9. Inspect valve seat for scoring or debris – clean or replace.
10. Reassemble, pressurise, and leak-test all connections.

## Torque Values
- Regulator cover bolts: 25–30 Nm
- Inlet/outlet connections: per manufacturer spec (typically 40–60 Nm)
